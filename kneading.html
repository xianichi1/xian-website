Why Do Cats Knead?

Cats knead by pushing their paws against soft surfaces, alternating between left and right. This behavior originates from kittenhood, as they knead their mother's belly to stimulate milk flow.

Understanding Kneading Behavior

Adult cats continue to knead as a comforting behavior. It can indicate contentment, mark territory through scent glands in their paws, or prepare a resting place.

How Should You Respond?

While kneading is normal, cats may extend their claws, which can be uncomfortable. To protect yourself, keep their nails trimmed or place a soft barrier between you and your cat. Provide alternative kneading surfaces like blankets or pillows.
